ANNA DI NATALE

ABOUT ME

Hi, I'm Anna!

I studied mathematics at the University of Bologna, in Italy. I am interested in applied mathematics, in particular to linguistics. Let me tell you why. I have always had a strong passion for literature and in general for everything that has to do with the act of writing. I have always loved writing mails and send them by post (even if it was already old fashioned when I started), I was into calligraphy before it become a trend, I love reading anything I can find and I notice and remember people's handwriting (my high school math teacher had the best handwriting I have ever seen - maybe that's why I chose to study mathematics at the University). I have always thought that my job would have been related to what I studied and that my free time would be massively dedicated to reading. I never thought that there could have been a way of connecting those two so different interests. Unexpectedly, during the first year of my Master's I discovered the existence of text analysis. It was so surprising because I expected mathematics to be applicable to anything that is in some way measurable and quantifiable. I never expected that maths could be applied to the most unquantifiable thing I knew: writing! That schocking finding changed my life. I got very exicted about it, and I still am.

If you want to find out more about myself, my interests and my research, just read ahead!

Welcome to my website. I am a mathematician and currently a PhD student at the Complexity Science Hub Vienna and the Medical University of Vienna, Section for Science of Complex Systems, CeMSIIS. Here you can find much useful information about me and my research.
